Pizza Margherita

Italian Pie Campania

Neapolitan pizza of tomato, mozzarella, basil, olive oil. The three colors of the Italian flag, baked at 480 C for 90 seconds in a wood-fired oven.

The story

The marketing story is that Raffaele Esposito of Pizzeria Brandi made this pizza for Queen Margherita of Savoy in 1889, naming it after her and arranging the toppings to match the Italian flag. The first part is documented; the flag claim was added later and is contested. What matters more is that Neapolitan pizza-makers had been pairing tomato and mozzarella on a dough for at least a century before 1889. The Margherita is the modern canonical version of an older Naples tradition. The Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana codified the rules in 1984: only San Marzano tomatoes, only fior di latte or buffalo mozzarella from Campania, only 60-90 second bakes in a wood oven at 430-480 C.

Variations

  • Margherita DOP. Strict AVPN: only San Marzano DOP tomatoes and Mozzarella di Bufala Campana DOP.
  • Margherita extra. Adds Parmigiano and a higher proportion of buffalo mozzarella.

How to make Pizza Margherita

Serves: 2 Prep: 24 hr Cook: 10 min

Ingredients

  • 500g (for 4 pizzas) 00 flour
  • 325ml, room temperature Water
  • 12g Fine sea salt
  • 2g (or 0.5g dried) Fresh yeast
  • 200g, crushed by hand, no cooking Whole San Marzano tomatoes
  • 200g, torn Fior di latte or buffalo mozzarella
  • 8-12 Fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp Extra virgin olive oil

Method

  1. Dissolve the yeast in the water. Combine with flour and salt; knead 10 minutes until the dough is smooth and slightly tacky.
  2. Cover and let rise at room temperature for 18-24 hours. The long cold fermentation builds the flavour profile.
  3. Divide into 4 balls (about 200g each). Rest covered for 4-6 more hours.
  4. Preheat a pizza stone or steel in the oven at the maximum temperature for at least 1 hour. A wood-fired oven is the real answer; a home oven at 250 C is the next best.
  5. Hand-stretch one ball into a 30cm round with a thicker rim (cornicione). No rolling pin.
  6. Top with crushed tomato (spread thin), then mozzarella in scattered pieces, then a small drizzle of olive oil. Basil goes on AFTER baking.
  7. Bake 60-90 seconds in a real Neapolitan oven, or 6-8 minutes at home until the rim puffs and the bottom is leopard-spotted.
  8. Tear fresh basil over the top. Eat immediately. The Margherita does not wait.

Editor's tip. The fermentation does the work. A 24-hour cold rise builds the flavour your oven cannot. Skip it at your peril.

Common mistakes

  • Rolling the dough out with a pin. Pins compress the gas; hand-stretching preserves the cornicione.
  • Putting basil on before baking. It burns. Always after.
  • Drowning the pie in sauce or cheese. Margherita is restraint.

Where to eat Pizza Margherita

  • L'Antica Pizzeria da Michele, Naples.
  • Pizzeria Starita a Materdei, Naples.
  • Pizzeria Brandi, Naples.
