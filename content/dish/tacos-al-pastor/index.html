Tacos al Pastor

Tacos al pastor

Mexican Main Mexico City

Mexico City pork tacos cooked vertical-spit shawarma-style, marinated in achiote, chiles, and pineapple. Served on small corn tortillas with onion, cilantro, lime, and a slice of charred pineapple.

The story

Tacos al pastor is the Mexican re-invention of Lebanese shawarma. Lebanese migrants settled in Puebla and Mexico City in the early 20th century and brought the vertical spit and marinated meat technique with them. By the 1960s the format was being made with pork (substituted for the original lamb) and a Mexican marinade of dried chiles, achiote, vinegar, and pineapple. The pineapple slice spinning on top of the trompo (spit) is the visual signature of the dish; the juice and acid help break down the pork and a thin sliver gets cut into each taco. Authentic tacos al pastor are made on a vertical gas-fired trompo, sliced thin, and served on hand-pressed corn tortillas the size of a child's hand.

Variations

  • Tacos arabes. Pueblan ancestor. Closer to the Lebanese original: pita-style bread, less aggressive chile marinade.
  • Vampiros. Open tortilla griddled with melted cheese before pastor goes on top.
  • Gringa. Quesadilla-style: pastor between two flour tortillas with cheese.

How to make Tacos al Pastor

Serves: 6 Prep: 12 hr Cook: 15 min

Ingredients

  • 1.5kg, thinly sliced Pork shoulder
  • 6, stemmed and seeded Dried guajillo chiles
  • 3, stemmed and seeded Dried ancho chiles
  • 2 tbsp Achiote paste
  • 1/4 cup White vinegar
  • 1/2 cup Pineapple juice
  • 6 cloves Garlic
  • 1 tsp, toasted Cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp Dried Mexican oregano
  • to taste Salt
  • 16 small (taco-size) Corn tortillas
  • 1/4 of a fruit, finely diced Fresh pineapple
  • 1/2, finely diced White onion
  • 1/2 bunch, chopped Cilantro
  • 4, in wedges Limes
  • to serve Salsa verde or salsa roja

Method

  1. Toast the chiles in a dry pan 30 seconds per side. Cover with hot water and soak 20 minutes.
  2. Drain. Blend with achiote, vinegar, pineapple juice, garlic, cumin, oregano, and salt to a smooth paste.
  3. Coat the pork slices completely. Marinate refrigerated at least 6 hours, ideally overnight.
  4. Without a trompo: lay marinated pork on a sheet pan and broil under high heat 4-5 minutes per side until edges char. Slice thinly.
  5. Char the pineapple in a hot dry pan until edges blacken. Dice finely.
  6. Warm tortillas on a dry comal for 15 seconds per side.
  7. Build each taco: meat, charred pineapple, onion, cilantro. Serve with salsa and lime.

Editor's tip. The doubled-up corn tortilla is not a typo. Authentic taco service uses two tortillas per taco so the bottom one absorbs juice and the top one stays intact.

Common mistakes

  • Using flour tortillas. Tacos al pastor are corn, full stop.
  • Skipping the pineapple. The fruit acid is half of the marinade story.
  • Cutting the pork in chunks. Vertical spit slices are paper-thin; replicate that at home with a sharp knife.

Where to eat Tacos al Pastor

  • El Vilsito, Mexico City.
  • Los Cocuyos, Mexico City.
  • El Tizoncito, Mexico City.
