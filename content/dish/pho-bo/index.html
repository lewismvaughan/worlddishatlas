Pho Bo

Phở bò

Vietnamese Soup Hanoi

Vietnamese beef noodle soup. Long-simmered beef-bone broth scented with charred ginger, onion, star anise, cloves, cinnamon. Rice noodles, thin-sliced raw beef, fresh herbs at the table.

The story

Pho emerged in northern Vietnam around the turn of the 20th century, most likely from street carts in the Nam Dinh and Hanoi area. French colonial demand for beef created a beef-bone supply the Vietnamese turned into a noodle soup. The northern (Hanoi) style is clear, restrained, deeply beefy with minimal garnish; the southern (Saigon) style that emerged after the 1954 partition is sweeter, herbier, and served with a side plate of basil, bean sprouts, sawtooth herb, lime, and chiles. Pho bo (beef) is the original; pho ga (chicken) followed in the 1930s.

Variations

  • Pho bo Hanoi. Northern style: restrained, no herb plate, lime and chile optional.
  • Pho bo Saigon. Southern style: served with a full herb plate, hoisin and sriracha at the table.
  • Pho ga. Chicken version. Faster broth (3-4 hours), shallot and ginger only.

How to make Pho Bo

Serves: 6 Prep: 30 min Cook: 8 hr

Ingredients

  • 2kg Beef knuckle and marrow bones
  • 500g Oxtail
  • 500g (whole piece) Brisket
  • 1 large hand, halved Ginger
  • 2 large, halved Yellow onions
  • 8 pods Star anise
  • 6 Whole cloves
  • 1 (5cm) Cinnamon stick
  • 1 pod Black cardamom
  • 1 tsp Coriander seeds
  • 3 tbsp Fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp Rock sugar
  • 400g Fresh banh pho rice noodles
  • 300g, frozen 30 min then sliced paper-thin Eye-round beef
  • 4, white and green separated, thin-sliced Scallions
  • 1 small bunch, chopped Cilantro
  • for the table (southern style) Thai basil, beansprouts, sawtooth herb, lime, Thai chiles

Method

  1. Char the ginger and onions over a flame or under the broiler until blackened on the cut sides. This is the flavour foundation.
  2. Toast the star anise, cloves, cinnamon, cardamom, and coriander in a dry pan 30 seconds. Tie in cheesecloth.
  3. Blanch the bones, oxtail, and brisket in boiling water for 10 minutes. Drain and rinse hard. This step removes scum that would cloud the broth.
  4. Return to a clean pot with 6L water, the charred aromatics, and the spice sachet. Bring to a simmer.
  5. Cook at the gentlest simmer for 6-8 hours. Skim foam every 30 minutes for the first 2 hours. Hard boil clouds the broth; resist it.
  6. Remove the brisket after 90 minutes; refrigerate. Continue simmering the bones for the full time.
  7. Strain the broth through cheesecloth. Season with fish sauce and rock sugar.
  8. Slice the cooled brisket thinly. Cook the fresh noodles in boiling water 30 seconds; drain into bowls.
  9. Top noodles with raw eye-round slices and sliced brisket. Pour the hot broth over (the raw beef cooks on contact). Garnish with scallion and cilantro.
  10. Serve with the herb plate on the side for southern style, or restrained for Hanoi style.

Editor's tip. Charring the ginger and onion is the single biggest flavour step. Skip it and you have brown water. Char them hard, until the cut faces are properly black.

Common mistakes

  • Boiling the broth hard. The whole point of pho is clarity; high heat clouds it irreversibly.
  • Adding hoisin or sriracha to a Hanoi-style bowl. That is southern (Saigon) practice; northern pho is meant to be tasted neat first.
  • Using thick rice noodles. Banh pho is flat and medium-thin; thick noodles change the dish.

Where to eat Pho Bo

  • Pho Gia Truyen, Hanoi.
  • Pho Thin Bo Ho, Hanoi.
  • Pho Hoa Pasteur, Ho Chi Minh City.
