Pad Thai

ผัดไทย

Thai Noodle Bangkok

Thai stir-fried rice noodles with tamarind, fish sauce, palm sugar, dried shrimp, egg, tofu, peanuts, and lime. National dish, but invented in the 1930s, not centuries old.

The story

Pad Thai is younger than most diners assume. Prime Minister Plaek Phibunsongkhram pushed it as a national dish in the late 1930s as part of a cultural reform programme that also gave Thailand its modern name. Rice noodles were promoted because they used less rice than steamed-rice dishes and reduced Thailand's dependence on rice imports during the war years. The canonical pad Thai pairs sweet (palm sugar), sour (tamarind), salty (fish sauce), umami (dried shrimp) in a balance that distinguishes it from Chinese stir-fries that share the same wok technique.

Variations

  • Pad Thai goong. With fresh river prawns. Common in tourist Bangkok, less common in home cooking.
  • Pad Thai ho mok. Wrapped in a thin omelette. A presentation flourish.

How to make Pad Thai

Serves: 2 Prep: 30 min Cook: 8 min

Ingredients

  • 200g, soaked in cold water 30 min Dried flat rice noodles
  • 3 tbsp (dilute concentrate with equal water) Tamarind paste
  • 2 tbsp Fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp, shaved Palm sugar
  • 1 tbsp, soaked and chopped Dried shrimp
  • 3 cloves, minced Garlic
  • 100g, cubed Firm tofu
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 cup Beansprouts
  • 1/2 cup, cut into 5cm lengths Garlic chives
  • 3 tbsp, crushed Roasted peanuts
  • 1, in wedges Lime
  • 2 tbsp Neutral oil

Method

  1. Make the sauce: combine tamarind, fish sauce, palm sugar in a small pan over low heat until the sugar dissolves. Reserve.
  2. Heat a wok to ripping-hot. Add the oil, then the garlic and dried shrimp; stir for 15 seconds.
  3. Add the tofu and stir-fry until edges crisp, about 1 minute.
  4. Drain the soaked noodles and toss them in. Pour the sauce over and stir-fry hard, lifting and tossing so noodles do not clump. Cook until noodles soften, about 2 minutes.
  5. Push everything to one side of the wok. Crack the eggs into the empty side and let set briefly, then break them up and fold into the noodles.
  6. Add the beansprouts and garlic chives. Toss for 30 seconds.
  7. Plate. Top with crushed peanuts. Serve with lime wedges, extra fish sauce, dried chili flakes, and sugar on the side so each diner adjusts to taste.

Editor's tip. Soak the noodles in cold water for half an hour. Hot soaking softens them too much for the wok. They should still be al dente when they hit the heat.

Common mistakes

  • Cooking in a non-hot pan. The wok must be smoking before noodles go in. Otherwise everything stews.
  • Using ketchup. Tamarind is the right acid; ketchup is the lazy substitute that ruins the dish.
  • Skipping the dried shrimp. The umami floor of pad Thai depends on it.

Where to eat Pad Thai

  • Thip Samai, Bangkok.
  • Pad Thai Fai Ta Lu, Bangkok.
